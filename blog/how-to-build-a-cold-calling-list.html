How to build a cold calling list

Cold Call X · Updated June 2026 · 5 min read

A great calling block starts the night before — with a list that's clean, segmented, and ready to dial. Spend thirty minutes here and you'll save hours of dead dials. Here's how to build one in a spreadsheet and get it into your phone.

1. Source the right numbers

Where you pull data depends on who you sell to:

  • Real estate: skip-tracing tools (PropStream, BatchLeads, DealMachine), county records, FSBO/expired feeds.
  • B2B: data providers like Apollo or ZoomInfo, LinkedIn exports, event and webinar lists.
  • Insurance & local services: aged-lead vendors, referral lists, past-quote follow-ups.

Whatever the source, you're responsible for compliance — scrub against the Do Not Call registry and follow the rules for your region and industry before you dial.

2. Structure the sheet for dialing

One row per lead, clear headers in the first row. A dialer-friendly layout looks like:

  • Name — first name at minimum; a real name makes openers warmer.
  • Phone — the primary number. Add Phone 2, Phone 3 for extra numbers rather than stacking them in one cell.
  • Email — for one-tap follow-ups.
  • Notes — source, address, or context you'll want on the call.
  • Tag — optional starting temperature or segment.

Keep numbers in a consistent format. Including the country/area code (e.g. +1 512 555 0142) avoids dialing errors when you import.

3. Clean before you call

Five minutes of cleanup pays off all session:

  • Remove duplicates and blank-number rows.
  • Standardize phone formatting so nothing fails to dial.
  • Split obviously different segments into separate lists (by market, product, or source) so you can work one at a time.

4. Import it into your dialer

Manually typing a list into your phone defeats the purpose. A good cold calling app imports your CSV or Google Sheet and auto-maps the columns, so Name, Phone, and Notes land in the right place. In Cold Call X you import a CSV or pull straight from Google Sheets, keep multiple lists with pinned favorites, and it handles multi-number leads — so an owner with three numbers stays one entry. From there you tap (or auto-dial) down the list.

5. Segment and prioritize

Before you start, decide the order. Filter to uncalled leads first, work the right timezone for the hour, and tag as you go so callbacks float to the top tomorrow. A list you can filter is a list you'll actually finish.

Takeaways

  • One row per lead; clean headers; extra numbers in their own columns.
  • Consistent phone formatting with country/area codes.
  • De-dupe and split into focused lists before you dial.
  • Import (don't retype) so columns auto-map — then filter and work it.
